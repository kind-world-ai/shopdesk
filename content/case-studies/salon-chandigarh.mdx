## The Problem: A Register, a Calculator, and Monthly Arguments

Priya Sharma has been running **Glamour Studio** in Sector 22, Chandigarh for 7 years. Five stylists. Services ranging from basic threading (₹80) to full bridal packages (₹15,000). Walk-ins and regulars from across Tricity.

For all 7 years, Priya tracked everything in a **physical register**. Every service, every client, every payment — handwritten. At the end of each month, she'd sit down with a calculator and spend **6+ hours** doing three things:

1. **Tallying each stylist's services** — flipping through 30 days of register entries, adding up who did what
2. **Calculating commission** — each stylist had a different rate (20% for juniors, 25% for seniors), with a ₹25,000 monthly target before commission kicks in
3. **Reconciling cash** — matching what the register says with what's actually in the drawer

### The Commission Dispute Problem

Every single month, at least one stylist would dispute their commission calculation.

> "Yeh service maine ki thi, Renu ne nahi ki."

> "March mein meri 47,000 ki services thi, aapne 42,000 likhi hain."

Priya couldn't prove anything because the register entries were sometimes unclear — especially on busy Saturdays when 3 stylists are working simultaneously and walk-ins are being assigned on the fly.

**The real cost:** Priya lost her best senior stylist in 2024. Meena left because she believed she was being under-credited for her services. Priya couldn't show her transparent records because transparent records didn't exist.

### No Idea of Monthly Profit

Ask Priya in January 2025: "What was your profit in December?" She couldn't answer. She knew roughly how much cash came in. She knew roughly what she paid in salaries. But product costs, rent proportions, GST liability — she had no consolidated view.

## The Solution: ShopDesk Salon CRM — 20 Minutes to Set Up

Priya heard about ShopDesk from another salon owner in Sector 35 who was already using it. She bought the Salon CRM for ₹999 in February 2025.

### Setup: What Took 20 Minutes

1. **Made a copy** of the Google Sheet to her own Drive — 2 minutes
2. **Entered her 5 staff names** in the Staff sheet — 3 minutes
3. **Set commission rates** — 20% for 2 juniors (above ₹20,000 target), 25% for 3 seniors (above ₹25,000 target) — 5 minutes
4. **Entered her service menu** with prices — haircut ₹500, colour ₹2,500, facial ₹600, bridal ₹15,000, etc. — 10 minutes

No app installation. No internet dependency after initial setup. Just a Google Sheet on her existing laptop at the salon counter.

### Daily Workflow Change

The only change to Priya's daily routine: instead of writing in the register, she (or her receptionist) enters each service in the ShopDesk Daily Log sheet.

Each entry takes **15 seconds**: select date, select stylist name (dropdown), select service (dropdown), enter amount. That's it.

On a typical day with 15–20 services, this takes about **5 minutes total** — roughly the same as writing in a register, just digital.

## The Results: First Month (March 2025)

### 1. Monthly Billing: 6 Hours → 30 Minutes

At the end of March, Priya opened the **Monthly Summary** sheet. Everything was already calculated:

- Each stylist's total services for the month
- Commission automatically applied based on rates and targets
- Base salary + commission = total payout per person

**Time spent on month-end billing: 30 minutes** — mostly reviewing the numbers and printing statements for each stylist to sign.

Previously: 6 hours of calculator work + cross-referencing register entries.

### 2. Commission Disputes: Eliminated

Every service entry has a **stylist name attached** from the moment it's logged. There's no ambiguity about who did what.

When Renu questioned one entry in March, Priya pulled up the Daily Log, showed her the exact date, service, client, and amount. Renu accepted it immediately — the data was clear.

No stylist has disputed their commission since ShopDesk was installed.

### 3. GST Filing: Actually Possible Now

The Monthly Summary automatically totals **taxable revenue by category**. Priya's CA now gets a single sheet with all the numbers he needs for GST filing — instead of Priya spending 2 hours compiling numbers from the register.

### 4. Profit Visibility: Knows by 2nd of Every Month

By the 2nd of April, Priya knew her March numbers:
- Total revenue: ₹3,47,000
- Staff costs (salary + commission): ₹78,400
- Product costs: ₹31,200
- Rent + utilities: ₹35,000
- **Net profit: ₹2,02,400**

She'd never had this clarity before. Ever.

## Key Takeaways for Salon Owners

1. **The register is the bottleneck.** Priya's salon wasn't disorganised — she was meticulous with her register. But paper registers don't calculate, don't summarise, and don't resolve disputes. A structured digital system does.

2. **Commission transparency retains staff.** Priya lost Meena because she couldn't prove the numbers. Transparent tracking doesn't just save time — it saves your best people.

3. **You don't need an app — you need a system.** ShopDesk isn't an app with login screens and servers. It's a Google Sheet with 11 interconnected sheets, 373 validated formulas, and a structure that makes salon management systematic.

4. **₹999 one-time is the right price for Indian salons.** Priya explored other salon software — most were ₹2,000–5,000/month. For a salon doing ₹3–4 lakh/month revenue, a monthly software subscription doesn't make sense. A one-time purchase does.
