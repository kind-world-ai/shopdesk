## Why Salon Management Matters

Running a salon in India is tough. Between walk-ins, bookings, staff management, inventory, and GST billing — most salon owners spend more time on paperwork than on their craft.

Here are 7 practical tips that successful salon owners in Chandigarh, Mohali, and across India use to run their businesses efficiently.

## 1. Track Every Appointment — Even Walk-ins

Most salons only track pre-booked appointments. But **walk-ins can be 40-60% of your revenue**. If you're not tracking them, you're missing crucial data about:

- Peak hours and slow periods
- Which services are most popular
- Staff utilisation rates

**Pro tip:** Use a simple Google Sheet to log every client — name, service, amount, stylist. ShopDesk Salon CRM does this automatically.

## 2. Automate Staff Commission Calculation

Commission disputes are the #1 reason stylists leave salons. If you're calculating commissions manually, errors are inevitable.

Set up a system where commissions are calculated automatically based on:

- Services performed
- Product sales
- Tips (if tracked)

This gives your staff confidence and saves you hours every month.

## 3. Know Your Revenue Per Chair Per Day

This is the most important metric for any salon. Calculate it as:

**Revenue Per Chair = Total Revenue ÷ (Number of Chairs × Working Days)**

If your revenue per chair is below ₹2,000/day, you have a utilisation problem. If it's above ₹4,000/day, you're ready to expand.

## 4. Track Inventory Before It Tracks You

Running out of hair colour on a Saturday? That's lost revenue. Over-ordering shampoo? That's dead money.

Track these for every product:

- Opening stock
- Purchases
- Usage (linked to services)
- Closing stock
- Reorder level

## 5. Send GST Invoices — Even If Not Required

Even if your salon is below the GST threshold, sending professional invoices builds trust. Clients who get proper bills:

- Refer more friends
- Perceive higher value
- Are less likely to haggle on prices

## 6. Review Your Numbers Weekly, Not Monthly

By the time you look at last month's numbers, it's too late to fix anything. Set aside 15 minutes every Saturday evening to check:

- This week's revenue vs last week
- Top 3 services by revenue
- Any staff with low booking rates
