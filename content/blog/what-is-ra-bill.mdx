## What is an RA Bill?

A **Running Account Bill (RA Bill)** is a progressive payment invoice raised by a contractor to the client at regular intervals — typically monthly — based on the work completed to date.

In Indian construction, RA Bills are the standard way contractors get paid. Instead of waiting until the entire project is complete, contractors raise intermediate bills as work progresses.

## How Does an RA Bill Work?

Here's the typical flow:

1. **Contractor completes work** for a billing period (usually 1 month)
2. **Quantity surveyor measures** the work done on-site
3. **RA Bill is prepared** with quantities × unit rates from the BOQ
4. **Deductions are applied** — advance recovery, retention, TDS
5. **GST is added** (typically 12% or 18%)
6. **Net payable amount** is calculated and submitted to the client

## RA Bill Calculation Formula

The standard RA Bill calculation in India follows this pattern:

- **Gross Amount** = Sum of (Quantity × Rate) for all items
- **Add GST** = Gross Amount × 12% (or 18%)
- **Less Advance Recovery** = Usually 20% of gross
- **Less Retention** = Usually 10% of gross (released after defect liability period)
- **Less TDS** = 2% under Section 194C
- **Net Payable** = Gross + GST − Advance − Retention − TDS

## Why Do Contractors Struggle with RA Bills?

Most contractors in Chandigarh, Mohali, and across Punjab still track RA Bills manually — either on paper or in basic Excel sheets. This leads to:

- **Calculation errors** in GST, TDS, and retention deductions
- **Delayed payments** because bills aren't submitted on time
- **No visibility** into cumulative billing vs contract value
- **Disputes** with clients over quantities and amounts

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between RA Bill and final bill?

An RA Bill is an interim payment based on work done so far. The **final bill** is raised after project completion and includes all remaining amounts, retention release, and final measurements.

### How often should I raise an RA Bill?

Most contracts specify **monthly** RA Bill submission. Some large projects may have fortnightly billing cycles. Check your contract terms.

### Is GST applicable on RA Bills?

Yes. GST is applicable on all construction services in India. The rate is typically **12% for government works** and **18% for private contracts**, depending on the type of work.
