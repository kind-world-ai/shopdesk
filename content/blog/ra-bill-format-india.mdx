## Quick Answer

The standard RA Bill format in India includes: **bill number, contract details, BOQ reference table** (description, unit, quantity, rate, amount), **previous cumulative quantities, current bill quantities, gross amount**, and **payment deductions** (GST 12%, retention, TDS 2% under Section 194C, advance recovery). The final line is **Net Payable** — the amount that actually reaches the contractor's bank account.

## What Fields Does a Standard RA Bill Include?

Every RA Bill accepted by clients — whether CPWD, PWD, or private — must contain these fields. Miss any one and your bill may be returned.

### Header Fields

| Field | Description | Example |

## RA Bill Format: BOQ Reference Table Structure

This is the core of every RA Bill — the work details linked to your BOQ. Here's the standard format with three sample items:

| Item No | Description | Unit | Contract Qty | Rate (₹) | Prev. Qty | Curr. Qty | Total to Date | Prev. Amount (₹) | Curr. Amount (₹) | Total Amount (₹) |
|---------|-------------|------|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1.1 | Earthwork excavation in all types of soil | Cum | 1,000 | 250 | 400 | 150 | 550 | 1,00,000 | 37,500 | 1,37,500 |
| 2.1 | RCC M25 grade including formwork and curing | Cum | 200 | 8,500 | 80 | 30 | 110 | 6,80,000 | 2,55,000 | 9,35,000 |
| 3.1 | Centering and shuttering (plywood, steel) | Sqm | 1,500 | 450 | 600 | 200 | 800 | 2,70,000 | 90,000 | 3,60,000 |
| | **Total** | | | | | | | **10,50,000** | **3,82,500** | **14,32,500** |

**Current Bill Gross Amount: ₹3,82,500**

> **How to read this:** The contractor has completed ₹14,32,500 worth of work to date. Of this, ₹10,50,000 was billed in previous RA Bills. The current bill is for the difference: **₹3,82,500**.

## Payment Summary Section Format

After the BOQ table, every RA Bill includes the payment waterfall. This calculates the actual amount the contractor receives.

Here's the standard format with a worked example using ₹5,00,000 as the gross amount:

| # | Description | Formula | Amount (₹) |
|---|-------------|---------|------------|
| A | Gross Amount of Work | Sum of all current bill items | 5,00,000 |
| B | Add: GST @ 12% | A × 12% | 60,000 |
| C | **Total including GST** | **A + B** | **5,60,000** |
| D | Less: Advance Recovery @ 20% | A × 20% | 1,00,000 |
| E | Less: Retention Money @ 10% | A × 10% | 50,000 |
| F | Less: TDS @ 2% (Section 194C) | A × 2% | 10,000 |
| **G** | **NET PAYABLE** | **C − D − E − F** | **4,00,000** |

**Out of ₹5,00,000 worth of work, the contractor receives ₹4,00,000 in hand.**

Where does the rest go?

- **₹60,000** → GST (contractor collects from client, remits to government)
- **₹1,00,000** → Advance recovery (repays mobilization advance given at project start)
- **₹50,000** → Retention (held by client as security, released after Defect Liability Period)
- **₹10,000** → TDS (client deposits to Income Tax dept, contractor claims credit in ITR via Form 26AS)

> **Important notes:**
> - GST rate varies: 12% for most construction, 18% for some services, 1–5% for affordable housing
> - Advance recovery stops once the full mobilization advance is repaid
> - Retention is usually capped at 5% of total contract value
> - TDS is on gross amount **excluding GST** (per CBDT Circular 23/2017)
> - Always check your specific contract for exact rates

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there a government-prescribed RA Bill format in India?

There is no single nationally mandated RA Bill format. However, **CPWD** (Central Public Works Department) has a standard format that most government departments follow. **State PWDs** have their own variations. Private contracts can use any format as long as it includes the essential fields: BOQ reference, cumulative quantities, payment waterfall, and certification signatures. The format shown in this article covers all fields required by CPWD, PWD, and private contracts.

### Can the RA Bill format be modified for my project?

Yes — within the terms of your contract. You can add extra columns (e.g., percentage of work completed, photographic evidence reference) but you cannot remove mandatory fields like BOQ quantities, rates, or payment deductions. Some clients require specific additional fields like deviation statements or quality test references. Always check your contract's billing clause before finalising your format.

### How do I number RA Bills?

Use sequential numbering starting from **RA-01** (or RA Bill No. 1). Each subsequent bill increments by one: RA-02, RA-03, and so on. The final bill after project completion is typically marked as **Final Bill** (not RA-XX). Some contractors use the format **RA/ProjectCode/01** for multi-project tracking — e.g., RA/MOH-127/03 for the third bill of project MOH-127.

### What is the validity of an RA Bill?

An RA Bill doesn't have a formal "validity period" like a quotation. However, most contracts specify a **payment timeline** — typically 30 days from submission and certification. If the client doesn't pay within this period, the contractor can issue a reminder and eventually a legal notice. The RA Bill itself remains a valid financial document for accounting and tax purposes indefinitely.
